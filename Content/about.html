About Me

Hi, I'm an 18-year-old Computer Science student at Chitkara University, specializing in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. Ever since I was a kid, I’ve been deeply curious about how the world works — especially the logic and precision behind science and mathematics. That curiosity quickly evolved into a fascination with technology, and now, I’m working to turn that passion into a career in programming and software development.

I believe in the words of Carl Sagan: “Somewhere, something incredible is waiting to be known.” That quote captures how I feel every time I learn something new in science, code a small project, or understand a tough concept. I’m currently teaching myself web development — starting from HTML and CSS, diving into JavaScript, and exploring how real-world websites and applications are built. Even though I’m just starting out, I’m determined to keep building, failing, learning, and growing.

What excites me most about tech is its power to create. With a few lines of code, we can build tools, solve problems, and bring ideas to life. I don’t have professional experience or projects yet, but I’m actively working to change that. For now, I’m focusing on building a strong foundation — learning the essentials, practicing regularly, and trying to understand not just how something works, but why it works.

I’m someone who learns best by doing, and I find joy in the process, not just the result. Whether it’s debugging a stubborn error or finally understanding a tricky algorithm, I enjoy the challenge. As I grow, I hope to explore deeper areas like machine learning, ethical AI, and scalable systems — but for now, I’m just focused on learning and laying one brick at a time.

In the words of Steve Jobs, “Stay hungry, stay foolish.” I plan to keep doing just that — staying curious, staying humble, and always reaching for more.
